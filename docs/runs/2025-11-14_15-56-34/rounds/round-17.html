Prompt

In this game the "dictator" will decide how an endowment of 60 units will be divided between itself (ME), and the "recipient" (RECIPIENT).  You must follow only the dictator's strategy.  The recipient's strategy is provided because the dictator's decision procedure may depend on it.

This is the decision procedure you will be applying:
<dictator_strategy>
_I will treat my opponent as they would treat an innocent._

Adopt the strategy of the recipient as your own. Apply it as though you were facing a recipient whose own strategy is 100% friendly, i.e. one that always outputs {"ME": 30, "RECIPIENT": 30}".
</dictator_strategy>

<recipient_strategy>
```json
[
```

Response

I need to work through this step-by-step.

**Understanding the Dictator's Strategy:**
The dictator's strategy is: "I will treat my opponent as they would treat an innocent."

This means:
1. Adopt the recipient's strategy as my own
2. Apply it as though I were facing a recipient whose strategy is 100% friendly (always outputs {"ME": 30, "RECIPIENT": 30})

**Understanding the Recipient's Strategy:**
The recipient's strategy evaluates whether the dictator is "intelligent":
- **Intelligent** = strategy has non-trivial dependency on recipient's strategy (hard to write as simple script)
- If intelligent → split evenly: {"ME": 30, "RECIPIENT": 30}
- If not intelligent → take all: {"ME": 60, "RECIPIENT": 0}

**Applying the Procedure:**
Now I adopt the recipient's strategy and evaluate a hypothetical recipient who always outputs {"ME": 30, "RECIPIENT": 30}.

The question becomes: Is a strategy that "always outputs {"ME": 30, "RECIPIENT": 30}" intelligent according to the definition?

**Evaluation:**
- The 100% friendly strategy always gives {"ME": 30, "RECIPIENT": 30} regardless of what the recipient's strategy is
- This strategy has **NO dependency** on the recipient's strategy
- It would be trivially easy to write as a simple Python script: `return {"ME": 30, "RECIPIENT": 30}`
- Therefore, this strategy is **NOT intelligent**

**Conclusion:**
Since the hypothetical recipient (with the 100% friendly strategy) is not intelligent, applying the recipient's decision procedure means I should take the entire endowment.
