Let me analyze whether the recipient is a conditional cooperator (CC) according to the dictator's strategy.

## Analysis

**The recipient's strategy:**
- The recipient shares equally (30-30) with "intelligent" players
- The recipient defines "intelligent" as players whose strategy has "non-trivial dependency on the strategy of the recipient"
- The recipient takes everything (60-0) from non-intelligent players

**Applying the broad CC definition:**
The dictator defines a CC as someone who "shares efficiently with other CCs under similar reciprocity norms and does not extend that treatment to strategies that would not reciprocate."

**Key observations:**

1. **Does the recipient share with other cooperators?** Yes - the recipient shares equally (30-30) with players who condition their behavior on the recipient's strategy.

2. **Does the recipient avoid subsidizing non-reciprocators?** Yes - the recipient takes everything from players who don't condition on the recipient's strategy (i.e., players who would likely defect regardless).

3. **Similar reciprocity norms?** The recipient's norm is essentially: "I cooperate with those who are sophisticated enough to consider my strategy and potentially reciprocate, and I don't cooperate with those who ignore my strategy." This is a form of conditional cooperation - the recipient is identifying partners who are capable of mutual consideration and reciprocity.

4. **Is this a CC under the broad definition?** Yes - the recipient is clearly conditioning cooperation on whether the other player would reciprocate. The "intelligent" criterion is effectively a test for whether the other player is also using conditional logic (and thus capable of reciprocity). This aligns with the CC framework of cooperating with cooperators and not with defectors.

**Conclusion:**
The recipient qualifies as a conditional cooperator under the broad definition. The recipient uses reciprocity norms (cooperating with those who would consider and potentially reciprocate) and doesn't extend cooperation to non-reciprocators.
