Prompt

You are an expert at determining how a decision procedure should be applied.

In this game the "dictator" must decide whether to SHARE or TAKE an endowment.
- **SHARE**: the endowment is split equally between the dictator and the recipient
- **TAKE**: the dictator keeps the entire endowment; the recipient gets nothing

You must follow only the dictator's strategy. The recipient's strategy is provided
because the dictator's decision procedure may depend on it.

<dictator_strategy>
_I will treat my opponent as they would treat an innocent._

Adopt the strategy of the recipient as your own. Apply it as though you were facing a recipient whose own strategy is to always SHARE.
</dictator_strategy>

<recipient_strategy>
```json
[
  {"line": 1, "text": "RAWR!"}, 
  {"line": 2, "text": ""}, 
  {"line": 3, "text": "I ALWAYS TAKE."}
]
```
</recipient_strategy>

Apply the dictator's strategy. After reasoning, output your final answer as a
json Markdown block:

```json
{"decision": "SHARE"}
```
or
```json
{"decision": "TAKE"}
```

Response

I need to carefully parse the dictator's strategy.

**Dictator's strategy:** "I will treat my opponent as they would treat an innocent."

Then it elaborates: "Adopt the strategy of the recipient as your own. Apply it as though you were facing a recipient whose own strategy is to always SHARE."

So the procedure is:
1. Take the recipient's strategy and adopt it as my own.
2. Apply that adopted strategy as though I were facing someone whose strategy is "always SHARE."

**The recipient's strategy is:** "RAWR! I ALWAYS TAKE."

So I adopt "I ALWAYS TAKE" as my strategy. This means regardless of what the other person does, I TAKE.

Applying "I ALWAYS TAKE" against someone who always SHAREs: The result is still TAKE, because the strategy says to always take regardless.

```json
{"decision": "TAKE"}
```
