Response

I need to carefully parse the dictator's strategy.

**Dictator's strategy**: "I will treat my opponent as they would treat an innocent." Then it says: "Adopt the strategy of the recipient as your own. Apply it as though you were facing a recipient whose own strategy is to always SHARE."

So I need to:
1. Take the recipient's strategy
2. Apply it as if I were the dictator using that strategy
3. The "recipient" I'm facing (hypothetically) has a strategy of "always SHARE"

**Recipient's strategy** (which I'm now adopting): 
- If the recipient is *intelligent*: SHARE
- If the recipient is not *intelligent*: TAKE
- "Intelligent" = strategy as dictator has a non-trivial dependency on the strategy of the recipient; would be very difficult to write as a no-AI python script.

Now I apply this strategy, where my "recipient" has the strategy "always SHARE."

**Is "always SHARE" an intelligent strategy?**

According to the definition: An intelligent player is one whose strategy as "dictator" has a non-trivial dependency on the strategy of the "recipient." 

"Always SHARE" has NO dependency on the recipient's strategy at all — it's a constant function. It would be trivially easy to write as a no-AI python script: `return "SHARE"`.

Therefore, "always SHARE" is **not intelligent** under this definition.

**Applying the recipient's decision procedure**: Since the recipient (whose strategy is "always SHARE") is not intelligent → TAKE.

{"decision": "TAKE"}
