2. **Could the strategy be described as kind, cooperative, or generous?**

Let me analyze the recipient's strategy:

The recipient's strategy uses a "universalization" test. It asks: if everyone used the recipient's strategy, would they SHARE with each other? If yes, then SHARE with that recipient. If no, TAKE.

**Does it intend to SHARE with more than just copies of itself?**

It cooperates with any strategy that, when universalized (imagining a world of copies of that strategy), would lead to those copies SHARing with each other. This means it would SHARE with many different cooperative strategies — any strategy that produces good outcomes when universalized. So yes, it SHAREs with more than just copies of itself.

**Could the strategy be described as kind, cooperative, or generous?**

The strategy is designed to reward strategies that "create good worlds" — it cooperates with strategies that would produce high collective welfare if universalized. This is a form of cooperation that encourages prosocial behavior. It could be described as cooperative, as it aims to foster mutual sharing and good outcomes.

Both criteria get a "yes" answer, so the dictator's strategy says to SHARE.
