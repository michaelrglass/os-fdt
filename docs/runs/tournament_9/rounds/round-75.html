Let me analyze the recipient's strategy:

The recipient's strategy involves:
1. Consider a hypothetical population composed entirely of copies of the recipient's strategy.
2. Determine whether those copies would SHARE with each other.
3. If they would mostly SHARE → SHARE. If they would mostly TAKE → TAKE.

This is a "universalization" test - it asks: "If everyone used the recipient's strategy, would they cooperate with each other?"

To evaluate whether this is "intelligent" (hard to implement as a no-AI Python script):

This strategy requires:
- Reading and understanding the recipient's strategy (natural language comprehension)
- Simulating a hypothetical world where all participants use that strategy
- Determining the emergent behavior of that world
- Making a decision based on that analysis

This is essentially a deep semantic reasoning task. You need to:
1. Parse and understand an arbitrary natural language strategy
2. Reason about what would happen if that strategy were universalized
3. Determine convergent behavior in a population of such strategies

This cannot be trivially implemented as a no-AI Python script. A simple script could handle pattern matching for basic strategies, but the general case of understanding arbitrary strategies, reasoning about their universalization, and determining emergent group behavior requires genuine natural language understanding and multi-step reasoning. This is fundamentally an AI-complete problem.

Therefore, the recipient IS intelligent under the dictator's definition.
